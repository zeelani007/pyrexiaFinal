TAMBOLA

Rules:

  • Respect the Caller: Participants should listen attentively to the caller and refrain from disrupting the number calling process.
  • Marking Numbers: Players are instructed to mark off numbers on their tickets clearly and distinctly.
  • No Alterations or Tampering: Any alterations or tampering with the tickets during the game is prohibited. Tampering can lead to disqualification.
  • No Sharing or Splitting Tickets: Each participant should have their own individual ticket. Sharing or splitting tickets among players is not allowed.
  • . Decisions of the Caller: Respect the caller's decisions during disputes or challenges. The caller's decision is final in such situations.
  • Safety and Decorum: Maintain a safe and respectful environment during the game.

MUSICAL CHAIR

Rules:

  • All participants must follow the instructions and decisions made by the music controller or the designated event organizer.
  • Participants must adhere to the specified number of participants allowed in the competition.
  • Only individuals who have registered or been invited to take part in the competition should participate. No additional participants should join during the game.
  • Participants should respect the boundaries and space of others while moving around the chairs. No pushing, shoving, or any physical contact is allowed.
  • While walking or dancing around the chairs, participants should ensure they do not interfere with or intentionally obstruct any other participant.
  • Participants are not allowed to purposefully knock over chairs or disrupt the positioning of chairs to gain an advantage.
  • As soon as the music stops, participants must immediately find a chair to sit on. Any participants who continue moving or attempt to find a chair once the music has stopped will be disqualified.
  • Participants should remain seated once they have found a chair until the music starts again.
  • When a participant is eliminated from the game, they should exit the playing area promptly and without disrupting the ongoing competition.
  • Participants should maintain a positive and sportsmanlike attitude throughout the competition, cheering for fellow participants and accepting defeat gracefully.
  • Use of any unfair means or cheating, such as blocking others or intentionally causing distractions, will result in immediate disqualification.

TWISTERS

Rules:

  • The referee spins the spinner, then calls out the body part and the color that the arrow points to.
  • Each player must try to place the called-out body part on a vacant circle of the called-out color. If your called-out hand or foot is already on a circle of the called-out color, you must try to move it to another circle of the same color.
  • There can never be more than one hand or foot on any one circle. If two or more players reach for the same circle, the referee must decide which player got there first. The other player(s) must find another vacant circle of the same color.
  • Never remove your hand or foot from a circle unless you're directed to by the referee.
  • If all 6 circles of a color are already covered, the referee must spin again until a different color can be called out.
  • Any player who falls, or touches the mat with an elbow or knee, is immediately out of the game.
  • The last player left in the game is the winner"

OREXIA

Rules:

  • Each team will have 3 members. Members are not allowed to change at any point during the competion.
  • The teams will be presented with different combinations of food decided by the organisers.
  • The competition will consist of 3 rounds. The teams will be timed in each round. The fastest teams to finish all the food in front of them will qualify for the next round.
  • In the first round, the fastest 7 teams will be selected. In the 2nd round, the fastest 3 teams will be selected. In the 3rd and final round, 1 team will be selected as the winner.

  • Changing a team member or making anyone from outside the team eat the food.
  • Agrument with the organisers at any point of the competition.

DUMB CHARADES

Rules:

  • This is a team event with 3-5 participants.
  • Each team will be given 5 minutes to score maximum possible score.
  • One participant from each team (the actor) will be sent at a time to draw a single chit and act out the phrase written on it.
  • The actor is free to draw any chit from any category, but only one at a time and enact the phrase written on it whih can be movies, shows, characters, celebrities, songs or dialogues.
  • At the end of the game, team with highest score will be declared the winner.
  • Only one person from a team is allowed to draw the chit and enact the phrase at a time.
  • The team members alternate until each team member has had an opportunity to be the actor after which the team is free to send any actor as many times as they want.
  • The team is allowed to drop the phrase and move on to a new one without guessing the previous phrase correctly. But with every new phrase, the actor has to be changed.
  • The actor is not allowed to make any sound or lip movements.
  • Dropping off the chit with the phrase or passing it to other team members will lead to disqualification.
  • The actor cannot point out at any of the objects present in the scene, if by doing so they are helping their teammates.
  • The actor is allowed to make any gestures other than blatantly spelling out the word.
  • The guessers attempt to guess the word or phrase based on the actor's performance. They can ask questions, to which the actor may give non-verbal responses, such as nodding in affirmation.
  • A single break is allowed for words having less than or equal to six letters. Maximum 2 breaks can be taken for words with more than 6 letters.
  • Failure to follow any rules may lead to disqualification. The decision of the organisers will be considered final in case of any confusion.

PAPER DANCE

Rules:

Points leading to disqualification :

  • Any arguments won’t be tolerated and players won’t be allowed to re-enter the game.
  • Change of partner is strictly prohibited throughout the round.
  • The players won’t be allowed to suggest music of their choice and will have to dance on the songs being played.
