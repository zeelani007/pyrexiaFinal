Monoact and Mime

Rules :

Mime is a unique and captivating form of non-verbal performance art that relies solely on body movements, gestures, facial expressions, and actions to convey emotions, tell stories, and communicate with the audience.

  • The performance must consist of a single act with no blackouts.
  • The participants must perform a monologue or any solo act from a script of their choice, with no limitations in the genre. However, if an adapted script is being performed, it is compulsory to give credits to the concerned person/group.
  • Duration of act should not exceed time limit i.e. 5 minutes.
  • The act should be in Hindi, English or Telugu. However, short passages in other languages are permitted.
  • If a second smaller character is required, it should be arranged by the participant themselves.
  • Participants can use props and a backing track as required.
  • Use of fire/explosives or any other potential harmful substances is strictly prohibited.
  • Obscenity (at the discretion of the judges) is not allowed and will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Decision of judges will be final and no objections will be entertained.
  • Round is subject to registrations.

    Emotional Quadrant

    Rules :

    Stage will be divided into 4 quadrant, each assigned with an emotion.

    • Each act will consist of 2 actors with a time limit of *minimum* 3min.
    • They will have to act according to the emotion that the quadrant is assigned to.
    • Each couple will be provided a topic , on spot , around which their act should revolve.
    • After each minute , actor will have to change the quadrant in which the were standing previously , to a newer quadrant.
    • Each couple will be given a time of 10 min after distribution of topics to prepare the act, failing which they will be disqualified.
    • Act should be performed only in hindi or english language
    • No props will be provided and use of props is optional and does not affect judging criteria.
    • Maximum time limit of the act is 6 min failing which points will be deducted.
    • Both the actors should not be present in the same quadrant at same time.
    • Decision of judges will be final and no objections will be entertained.

    EMOTIONAL QUADRANT JUDGING CRITERIA –

    • Flow of conversation
    • Humor
    • Interruption
    • Coordination
    • Time limit
